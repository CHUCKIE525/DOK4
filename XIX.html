Amendment
  XIX

The right of citizens to vote will not be denied by the government based on gender.

    Minor v. Happersett - In 1872 Virginia Minor, an officer of the National Women Sufferage Association was stopped from registering to vote in St. Lewis. Stating that she was denied one of the “privileges and immunities of citizenship” given to her by the 14th amendment, she and her husband sued the voting officer, Reese Happersett. The case went to the supreme court but was ruled unanimously that women’s voting rights were not protected by the 14th amendment.
