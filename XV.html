Amendment
  XV

The right of citizens to vote will not be denied by the government based on race, color, or previous condition.

    United States v. Reese - In 1873 two election officers, Hiram Reese and Matthew Foushee, in Lexington, Kentucky stopped William Garner, an African American man from voting in a municipal election. They claimed that he “had failed to pay a tax of $1.50,” but in reality, Garner tried to pay the tax and was refused by the tax collector. The Enforcement Act of 1870, which described penalties for violations of the 15th amendment, stated that if an officer stopped someone from voting then that person could present them with an affidavit that would qualify them. The two officers didn’t accept Garner’s affidavit and were charged with violating the Enforcement Act. They were not found guilty as the relevant sections of the Enforcement Act were not enough to justify a conviction.
